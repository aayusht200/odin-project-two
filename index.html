My take on some unique cafés I visited in NYC!
From cozy corners with soft lighting to fast-paced espresso bars, I explored a variety of coffee spots across the city. Here's what I tasted, saw, and loved — and what you should try if you’re ever in town.
Café Highlights

Cafe Ambrosia

A calm, sunlit space with Greek-French minimalist decor and a relaxed, work-friendly vibe. The vanilla latte was smooth and earthy. Don’t miss their famous tiramisu latte!

10000 Coffee

A sleek, modern café near Times Square. Limited seating but perfect for a quick pick-me-up. The vanilla latte was creamy, and the strawberry cream latte felt like dessert in a cup.

A Japanese-inspired espresso bar with futuristic design and no seating. The iced Kyoto latte was sweet and smooth. Try their espresso tonic for something refreshingly unique.

Pull Tab

A quirky gray-toned café from China near Bryant Park. The iced orange americano was bold, and their signature aerofoam coffee had a creamy, evolving taste. Don’t skip the butter toast!

Every cup tells a story — and in NYC, each one has its own vibe.
