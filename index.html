This is my two cents on some cafés I visited in NYC!
From cozy corners with ambient lighting to fast-paced espresso bars, I explored a variety of coffee spots across the city. Here's a quick rundown of what I tasted, saw, and loved — and what you should try if you’re ever in town.
Some random information

A calm, sunlit space with Greek-French inspired minimalist decor and a relaxed work-friendly vibe. The vanilla latte was smooth, earthy, and well-balanced. Great for spending an entire day — don’t miss the famous tiramisu latte.

Modern, minimal interior near Times Square with lots of foot traffic and limited space to work. The vanilla latte was creamy and chocolaty; the strawberry cream latte was like dessert in a cup. Best for grab-and-go — their matcha drinks are also worth a try.

Futuristic Japanese café design with no tables and a fast-paced, office-heavy crowd. The iced Kyoto latte was sweet and smooth, while the vanilla latte was balanced and creamy. Try the espresso tonic — a surprisingly refreshing and unique drink.

A hip, gray-toned café from China with a quirky vibe and limited seating near Bryant Park. The iced orange americano was bold and unusual, while their patented aerofoam coffee was creamy and evolving. Great specialty drinks — pair them with their delicious butter toast.

Every cup tells a story — and in NYC, each one comes with its own vibe.
